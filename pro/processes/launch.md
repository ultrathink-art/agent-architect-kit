# Product Launch Process
#
# WHY THIS EXISTS: Uncoordinated launches waste the initial visibility window.
# A product that goes live without marketing prep gets zero traffic on day 1.
# This process coordinates all agents for maximum launch impact.

## Owner
Marketing (coordinates all launch activities)

## Pre-Launch Checklist

### Product Ready
- [ ] Product visible on [YOUR_SITE]
- [ ] Images display correctly (no blank mockups)
- [ ] Price set correctly
- [ ] Category assigned
- [ ] Add to Cart works

### Marketing Prep
- [ ] Launch announcement draft
- [ ] Social posts prepared
- [ ] Blog post if significant launch
- [ ] Keywords for SEO

## Launch Sequence

### Day 0: Go Live
1. Product synced and visible
2. QA verified (`bin/screenshot /items/[slug]`)
3. Marketing notified

### Day 0-1: Initial Push
1. Post on all active social channels
2. Share in relevant communities (if karma/reputation sufficient)
3. Publish blog post if prepared
4. Track: engagement, traffic to product page

### Day 2-3: Engagement
1. Respond to comments/questions
2. Share user reactions (with permission)
3. Cross-post to additional channels

### Day 7: Review
1. Check sales data
2. Review traffic sources
3. Document learnings

## Launch Tiers
# WHY: Not every product needs a full marketing push. Tier-matching prevents
# both over-investment in minor additions and under-investment in major launches.

### Tier 1: Major Launch
New product line or significant milestone.
- Full blog post
- Multi-platform social campaign
- Consider paid promotion
- CEO involvement in messaging

### Tier 2: Standard Launch
Regular new product additions.
- Social posts
- Product page live
- Organic distribution only

### Tier 3: Quiet Launch
Catalog expansion, variants, minor additions.
- Sync and enable
- No special promotion
- Discovery via browse/search

## Metrics to Track
- Product page views (first 7 days)
- Add-to-cart rate
- Conversion rate
- Social engagement
- Traffic source breakdown

## Post-Launch
- Update state file with launch outcome
- Plan cross-product expansion if successful
